GIRAFFE

The giraffe (Giraffa camelopardalis) is the tallest land animal on Earth, native to the African savannah.

Giraffes use their long necks to reach leaves high up in trees, mainly feeding on acacia leaves.

Their distinctive coat patterns help with camouflage and temperature regulation.

Despite their height, giraffes are graceful animals and can run up to 60 km/h in short bursts.
